Muhammad Yunus:

In 2006, Muhammad Yunus, an economist from Bangladesh, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of his efforts to combat global poverty and empower millions through a social innovation now known as micro-finance. In this GROOC interview, he shares his experience with scaling the Grameen Bank and measuring its social impact, as well as his thoughts about leadership, social imagination and human connection.



Muhammad Yunus is the Grameen founder. He was born on 28th June, 1940 in the village of Bathua, Chittagong, the business centre of what was then Eastern Bengal. He was the 3rd of 14 children of whom five died in infancy. His father was a successful goldsmith. He always encouraged his sons to seek higher education.
