Welcome to Unit 1!

In this unit, we will consider the history of educational technology, introduce the theory of constructionism, and look at two projects inspired by it. Our first live event, a Reddit "Ask Me Anything" with Professor Klopfer, will take place on Thursday, February 4, 2016 at 3 PM UTC. You will receive access information prior to the event. 

  • History of Educational Technology - We will explore how technologies used in education have (or have not) changed the content and procedure of learning and teaching.
  • Constructionism & Related Educational Theories: Professor Susan Yoon discusses where the theory of constructionism came from and how it relates to the design of current educational technology. 
  • Logo: Professor Hal Abelson presents a case study of an early programming language that enabled students to work with computers in a different way.
  • Scratch Programming: Professor Mitch Resnick and Ricarose Roque tell us about a modern-day descendant of Logo and give examples of how it is used. 

We will be reading "Most Influential Theories of Learning" (a brief overview put together by UNESCO) and a section of the U.S. Department of Education Ed Tech Developer's Guide: A Primer for Software Developers, Startups, and Entrepreneurs.Participants who wish to dive deeper may read Edith Ackermann's paper ""Piaget’s Constructivism, Papert’s Constructionism: What’s the difference?."
